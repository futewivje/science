Introduction to BHA

What is BHA?

BHA (Butylated hydroxyanisole) is a synthetic antioxidant used in many everyday products. It is made up of a mixture of two isomers, both sharing the molecular formula C11H16O2.

Structure and Function

The structure of BHA includes a ring with a hydroxyl group, which is important in its ability to prevent oxidation. Oxidation occurs when fats and oils break down due to exposure to oxygen, leading to unpleasant odors, flavors, and decreased shelf life.

How Does BHA Work?

BHA works by neutralizing free radicals, which are unstable molecules that trigger oxidation. This makes BHA highly valuable in preserving the quality and safety of items, including food, cosmetics, and medications.
